Smoked Salmon Runbook v2.0

Derrick’s Hybrid Super Smoke Method

Objective

Produce moist, flavorful smoked salmon with noticeable smoke flavor, bright lemon notes, and a lightly firmed exterior using a Traeger smoker and butcher paper boat.

Target Internal Temp: 135-140°F

Total Cook Time: ~2 to 2.5 hours

Pellets: B&B Championship Blend

Ingredients

Salmon

  • 1 salmon fillet (skin on)

Seasoning

  • Kosher salt
  • Black pepper
  • Garlic powder

Citrus

  • 1 lemon

    • Half sliced
    • Half reserved for juice

Binder

  • Light olive oil

Equipment

  • Traeger smoker
  • Meat probe
  • Butcher paper
  • Spray bottle (optional)

Phase 1 - Preparation

8:30 AM

Prep Salmon

  • Pat salmon dry

  • Light coat of olive oil

  • Season with:

    • Salt
    • Pepper
    • Garlic powder

Prepare Lemon

  • Slice lemon into thin rounds
  • Reserve remaining half for juice

Phase 2 - Build Butcher Paper Boat

Purpose

Creates a moisture-retaining cooking vessel while allowing smoke exposure.

Method

  1. Tear off large sheet of butcher paper
  2. Fold edges upward creating shallow tray
  3. Place several lemon slices underneath salmon
  4. Place salmon skin-side down
  5. Add remaining lemon slices on top
  6. Fold sides slightly upward

Do NOT seal.

Think:

Brisket Boat
      +
Salmon Tray

Open top.

Open ends.

Smoke can still circulate.

Phase 3 - Smoke Session

Stage A - Flavor Build

9:00 AM

Smoker Settings:

  • 225°F
  • Super Smoke ON

Cook:

  • 60 minutes

Optional:

  • Light lemon-water spritz at 30 minutes

Goal:

  • Build smoke flavor
  • Begin gentle cooking

Stage B - Finish Cook

10:00 AM

Increase temperature:

  • 325°F

Continue cooking:

  • 45-60 minutes

Begin checking temperature around:

  • 10:45 AM

Target:

  • 135-140°F

Phase 4 - Surface Firming

Optional

If salmon surface appears wet:

Open boat slightly.

Continue:

  • 10-15 minutes
  • 325-350°F

Goal:

  • Lightly firm surface
  • Slight caramelization
  • Maintain moisture

Do NOT chase crispy skin.

Phase 5 - Rest

11:15-11:30 AM

Remove salmon.

Leave in butcher paper boat.

Rest:

  • 10-15 minutes

Benefits:

  • Juices redistribute
  • Carryover cooking completes
  • Texture improves

Phase 6 - Serve

Finish

Squeeze fresh lemon juice over salmon.

Optional garnish:

  • Fresh dill
  • Cracked black pepper
  • Lemon wedges

Quick Reference Card

Stage Temp Time
Super Smoke 225°F 60 min
Finish Cook 325°F 45-60 min
Optional Firming 325-350°F 10-15 min
Rest Off Smoker 10-15 min

Pull Temperature

135-140°F

Lessons Learned

Use When

✅ Family dinner

✅ Fresh salmon fillets

✅ Want smoke flavor without an all-day cook

✅ Want moisture retention

Avoid

❌ Wrapping tightly in foil

❌ Cooking above 145°F internal

❌ 3+ hour low-temp sessions unless making traditional smoked salmon
