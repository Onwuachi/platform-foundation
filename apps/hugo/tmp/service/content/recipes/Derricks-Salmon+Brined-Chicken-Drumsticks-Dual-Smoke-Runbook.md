# 🔥 KB: Salmon + Brined Chicken Drumsticks Dual Smoke Runbook


## Overview

This runbook covers a **staggered smoker workflow** where:

* Salmon is smoked low and slow first
* Brined chicken drumsticks are introduced later at higher heat
* Both finish together with a final crisp phase at 325–350°F

This method ensures:

* Salmon stays moist and tender
* Drumsticks are safely cooked from thawed state
* Both proteins finish at the same time

---

## 🧂 Stage 0: Drumstick Brine (Prep Phase)

### Ingredients

* Cold water (enough to submerge drumsticks)
* Salt (basic brine ratio: ~1–2 tbsp per quart of water)

### Steps

1. Submerge drumsticks in salt brine
2. Brine for **20–30 minutes max**
3. Remove and rinse lightly (optional)
4. Pat completely dry

### Finish Prep

* Coat lightly with oil or melted butter
* Apply dry rub (paprika, garlic powder, pepper, BBQ rub)

> ⚠️ Critical: Dry surface = better crisp at high heat

---

## 🔥 Stage 1: Salmon Smoke Phase

### Smoker Setup

* Temperature: **225°F**
* Wood: Apple / Cherry (best for salmon + poultry combo)

### Steps

1. Preheat smoker to 225°F
2. Place salmon on smoker
3. Smoke until internal temp reaches:

   * **125–130°F (pull point before finish crisp)**
4. Do NOT add chicken yet

### Duration

* ~30–45 min depending on salmon thickness

---

## 🔁 Stage 2: Transition + Drumstick Load

### Trigger Point

When salmon reaches ~125–130°F internal:

### Actions

1. Increase smoker temp to **325–350°F**
2. Add brined + seasoned drumsticks
3. Reposition salmon if needed for even heat

---

## 🔥 Stage 3: Final Cook + Crisp Phase

### Targets

* Salmon:

  * Finish texture + skin crisp
  * Final internal: ~130–140°F (preferred doneness range)

* Drumsticks:

  * Cook to **165°F internal minimum**

### Timing

* Drumsticks: ~20–30 min at high heat
* Salmon: ~5–10 min final crisp phase

---

## 🧯 Stage 4: Rest & Serve

1. Remove both proteins once targets are hit
2. Rest:

   * Salmon: 3–5 min
   * Drumsticks: 5–10 min
3. Serve together

---

## ⚡ Key Rules (Do Not Skip)

* Do NOT add drumsticks before salmon reaches ~125°F
* Always dry drumsticks after brine
* Always verify chicken with thermometer (165°F minimum)
* Higher heat phase is intentional — don’t fear 325–350°F

---

## 🧠 Why This Works

* Salmon gets controlled smoke exposure before heat spike
* Drumsticks avoid over-smoking and get direct high-heat finish
* Single temp ramp = synchronized finish timing

---

## Optional Enhancements

* Glaze drumsticks with BBQ sauce in last 5–10 min
* Add butter + honey glaze to salmon during crisp phase
* Use probe thermometers for both proteins simultaneously
