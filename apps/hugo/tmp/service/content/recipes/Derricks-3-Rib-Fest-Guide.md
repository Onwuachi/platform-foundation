## 🍖 Derrick's 3-Rib Fest Pitmaster Runbook



🎛️ The Core Philosophy

* Ditch the Drudgery: No more 12-hour low-and-slow stall marathons.
* Controlled Heat Push: Operating at 265°F–275°F delivers better bark and rendering.
* Adhesion, then Acceleration: Short 225°F window for smoke, then push the gas.

![Derricks-3-Rib-Fest-Guide](/images/Derricks-3-Rib-Fest-Guide.png)

------------------------------
## 📅 Night Before: Prep & Dry Brine

* Beef Plate Ribs: Coat heavily with kosher salt, coarse pepper, and garlic.
* Pork Rack: Apply light salt only (pre-enhanced), pepper, garlic, and optional rosemary/thyme.
* Baby Backs: Apply standard rib rub (paprika, pepper, garlic, light brown sugar).
* Rest: Place all meats uncovered on wire racks in the fridge overnight.

------------------------------
## ⏱️ Master Timeline & Load Order

[07:30 AM] ── Beef Plate Ribs On (225°F)
[08:15 AM] ── Heat Push to 265°F-275°F
[09:00 AM] ── Pork Rack On
[10:00 AM] ── Baby Backs On
[11:30 AM] ── Baby Back Glaze (Tack Phase)
[12:00 PM] ── Wrap Baby Backs (Butcher Paper)
[12:30 PM] ── Wrap Beef Ribs (Butcher Paper)
[01:30 PM] ── Pork Rack Pull & Rest (145°F-150°F)
[03:30 PM] ── Baby Backs Pull & Rest (~195°F-200°F)
[04:00 PM] ── Beef Ribs Pull & Rest (200°F-205°F)
[05:00 PM] ── 🍽️ SERVE TIME

------------------------------
## 🪵 Phase 1: Ignition & Smoke Adhesion

* 05:00 AM: Hit the gym while the dry brine finishes its work.
* 07:15 AM: Fire up the pellet grill with Kirkland pellets to 225°F.
* 07:30 AM: Load Beef Plate Ribs into the hottest zone once grill hits 200°F+. Do not wait for "perfect."
* 08:15 AM: Immediately step temp up to 265°F–275°F. (End of smoke adhesion window).

------------------------------
## 🌡️ Phase 2: Staggered Loading & Management

* 09:00 AM: Load Pork Rack. Insert probe into the thickest center zone, avoiding the bone.
* 10:00 AM: Load Baby Back Ribs.
* 11:30 AM: Glaze Baby Backs ONLY with a very light coat of Diana sauce + vinegar + seasoning blend.

------------------------------
## 📦 Phase 3: The Butcher Paper Wrap

* 12:00 PM: Wrap Baby Backs in pink butcher paper with a touch of glaze and butter/tallow. No heavy sugar baths.
* 12:30 PM: Wrap Beef Plate Ribs when bark is dark mahogany and doesn't wipe off. Optional: Add a splash of beef tallow.
* Pork Rack Note: Leave unwrapped. Only tent loosely with paper if the exterior color gets too dark near the finish.

------------------------------
## 🏁 Target Finish & Pull Parameters

| Meat Cut | Pull Temp | Physical Target Indicator | Rest Time |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pork Rack | 145°F – 150°F | Juicy, pork prime rib texture | 30+ Mins (Foil tent) |
| Baby Backs | 195°F – 200°F | Clean bend test; bone exposure | 30–60 Mins (In paper) |
| Beef Plate | 200°F – 205°F | Probe tender (like warm butter) | 60+ Mins (Cooler/Oven) |

------------------------------
## 🧠 Troubleshooting & KB Notes

* Grill Temp Fluctuations: Trust the process. Anywhere between 265°F and 285°F is completely fine for these cuts.
* The Pork Rack Finish: It will finish first. Keep it warm by tenting with foil on the counter—do not let it overcook.
* The Beef Rib Stall: If the beef stalls hard around 175°F, verify the bark is fully set, wrap tightly, and don't be afraid to push the pit to 285°F to cross the finish line.
