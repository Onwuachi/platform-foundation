# Why I Bought It

Bourbon County Brand Stout is one of the few beers I purchase every year without hesitation.

It's become an annual holiday tradition and serves as my personal benchmark for bourbon barrel-aged imperial stouts. Each new vintage gives me the opportunity to revisit a beer that has consistently delivered exceptional balance between rich roasted malt and authentic bourbon barrel character.

Even after trying many other barrel-aged stouts, this remains one of the releases I look forward to the most each fall.

---

# Personal History

My wife and I visited the original Goose Island brewpub in Chicago years before I became seriously interested in bourbon.

Looking back, that trip became one of the earliest memories in what eventually grew into both my craft beer hobby and my bourbon journey.

I've collected every Bourbon County Brand Stout release since 2019, making it one of the longest-running traditions in my beer collection.

# Appearance

Nearly opaque black with a dark mocha head that fades into a persistent ring. Thick, oily legs coat the glass, immediately hinting at the beer's rich body and barrel influence.

---

# Aroma

Layers of bourbon-soaked oak, vanilla, roasted coffee, dark chocolate, caramel, toffee, and rich malt rise from the glass. The bourbon is immediately noticeable but never overwhelming, allowing the roasted character to remain front and center.

---

# Flavor

The first sip is remarkably balanced.

Coffee, cocoa, caramel, toffee, roasted malt, dark chocolate, and subtle dark fruit all work together with a beautifully integrated bourbon barrel character.

Rather than tasting like bourbon poured into stout, the whiskey barrel character is completely integrated into the beer. Vanilla, toasted oak, spirit warmth, roasted malt, coffee, chocolate, and caramel all evolve together without any single flavor dominating the palate.

This balance is what separates Bourbon County from many barrel-aged competitors.

---

# Mouthfeel

Full-bodied, silky, and creamy texture.

Excellent viscosity without becoming syrupy.

---

# Finish

Long, warming, and satisfying.

Roasted coffee, bittersweet chocolate, oak tannins, vanilla, and lingering bourbon slowly fade while inviting another sip.

---

# Final Thoughts

Some beers are memorable because they're rare.

Bourbon County Brand Stout belongs firmly in the second category.

It helped introduce me to bourbon barrel-aged beer, eventually influenced my interest in bourbon itself, and has become one of my annual holiday traditions.

Looking back, it's easy to see that Bourbon County became the bridge between my craft beer journey and the bourbon collection I've built today.

Even after exploring dozens of barrel-aged stouts over the years, this remains the one I look forward to every winter.

For me, it isn't simply a great beer.
