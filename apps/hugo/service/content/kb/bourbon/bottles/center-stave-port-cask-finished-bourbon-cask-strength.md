# Why I Bought It

Finished bourbons can be hit or miss.

Too often the finishing cask becomes the entire story, masking the bourbon underneath with excessive sweetness or wine influence.

Center Stave Port Cask Finished Bourbon Cask Strength caught my attention because it promised a higher-proof, port-finished bourbon at an approachable price. I wanted to know whether the port finish would complement the bourbon—or overwhelm it.

Fortunately, it landed much closer to the former.

# Appearance

A rich copper color with deep amber highlights.

Excellent viscosity with slow-moving legs that suggest a full-bodied pour.

---

# Nose

The first surprise is what *isn't* there.

Despite the port finish, the wine influence stays remarkably restrained.

Instead, I found:

- Oak
- Vanilla
- Caramel
- Dark cherry
- Light baking spice

Everything feels well integrated.

The proof is also exceptionally well hidden, drinking softer than its 106 proof would suggest.

---

# Palate

This is where the bourbon really shines.

The mouthfeel is rich, creamy, and slightly oily without becoming heavy.

Notes I picked up include:

- Caramel
- Dark cherry
- Oaky sweetness
- Black pepper
- Vanilla

The pepper provides structure without overpowering the palate, while the oak remains one of the dominant characteristics.

To me, it drinks more like an 8-10 year bourbon than its stated minimum age would suggest.

---

# Finish

Long, warm, and slightly sweet.

Unlike Wild Turkey Rare Breed, which finishes drier and more spice-forward, Center Stave leans into a softer sweetness while allowing the oak and caramel to linger.

The port influence never becomes overly wine-like.

---

# What I Liked

- Excellent overall balance
- Port finish complements rather than dominates
- Rich, creamy mouthfeel
- Well-hidden proof
- Oak remains the centerpiece
- Dark cherry adds complexity without becoming overly fruity
- Outstanding value for the price

---

# What I'd Change

Very little.

If anything, I would welcome a bit more oak from additional aging, but the current profile is already remarkably polished for the price.

---

# Food Pairings

- Smoked pork loin
- Prime rib
- Cherry-glazed ham
- Dark chocolate
- Bread pudding
- Pecan pie

---

# Where It Fits

This bottle fits surprisingly well alongside some of my favorite Bottled-in-Bond bourbons.

While the profiles are different, I found myself thinking about:

- Heaven Hill Bottled-in-Bond
- Bardstown Bottled-in-Bond
- Bondstone Double Oak

What they share is balance.

The finishing cask adds another layer of flavor without distracting from the underlying bourbon.

Unlike some port-finished bourbons that become overly wine-forward, Center Stave keeps the bourbon as the main attraction.

---

# Final Thoughts

Center Stave exceeded my expectations.

I expected the port cask to dominate the whiskey, but instead it simply rounded out the bourbon with subtle notes of dark fruit and sweetness.

The result is a bourbon that feels mature, drinks below its proof, and offers excellent balance throughout the entire experience.

For under $45, it's one of the better values I've found in the finished bourbon category.

It's a bottle I'd gladly recommend to someone looking to explore finished bourbons without sacrificing classic bourbon character.
