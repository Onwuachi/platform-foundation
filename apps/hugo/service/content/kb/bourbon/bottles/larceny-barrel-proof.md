# Why I Bought It

Larceny Barrel Proof has become one of the wheated bourbons I keep coming back to, and Batch A125 was an easy pickup at **under $40**.

At 125 proof, this is a serious bourbon on paper, but the actual drinking experience is much more approachable than the proof suggests. The combination of a creamy texture, sweet cherry and caramel notes, integrated pepper spice, and well-hidden heat makes this a dangerously drinkable bourbon.

The price makes it even more compelling.

At under $40, this is an exceptional value for a barrel-proof wheated bourbon that I rate a **9/10**.

# Appearance

Rich amber with a warm, inviting color.

A125 is noticeably **lighter than my Total Wine Private Barrel B524**, which presents as a deeper, darker amber.

A125 still looks rich and mature, but the color reflects the difference in barrel character between the two expressions.

---

# Nose

Creamy and sweet with notes of:

* Cherry
* Caramel
* Sweet oak
* Rock candy
* Light pepper spice

The nose gives hints of the proof, but the alcohol doesn't overwhelm the experience.

There is a lot going on beneath the surface, and the bourbon continues to reveal itself as you spend more time with it.

---

# Palate

This is where A125 really shines.

**Creamy. Viscous. Sweet.**

The first impression is rich and coating, with flavors of:

* Cherry
* Caramel
* Rock candy / hard candy
* Sweet oak
* Pepper
* Warming bourbon spice

The **pepper is extremely well integrated**. It is present without becoming sharp or aggressive.

The heat is also remarkably well hidden for a 125-proof bourbon.

The oak contributes depth without becoming bitter or overly tannic. Instead of dominating the palate, it works with the cherry and caramel sweetness.

One of the things I enjoy most is how the bourbon **changes as you taste it**. Different flavors emerge at different points, making each sip a little different from the one before it.

There is a lot happening here.

---

# Mouthfeel

**Extremely creamy and viscous.**

The bourbon coats the palate with a thick, rich texture that carries the flavor for a long time.

This is one of the defining characteristics of A125 for me.

The combination of viscosity and sweetness makes it exceptionally easy to drink despite the proof.

---

# Finish

Long, warming, and slightly dry.

The finish brings lingering notes of:

* Cherry
* Caramel
* Pepper
* Sweet oak
* Warming alcohol

The slight dryness is particularly interesting.

It keeps the bourbon from becoming overly sweet and almost acts as a reset for the palate.

Unfortunately, it also creates a dangerous side effect:

**It makes you want another sip.**

The bourbon is creamy and sweet, then the slight dryness arrives and invites you right back in.

At 125 proof, that is a combination worth respecting.

---

# Overall Impression

Larceny Barrel Proof Batch A125 is an **exceptionally well-balanced high-proof wheated bourbon**.

The proof is substantial, but the bourbon doesn't drink like a 125-proof whiskey.

Instead, it is:

* Creamy
* Viscous
* Sweet
* Rich
* Complex
* Well integrated

The cherry and caramel sweetness are supported by rock candy notes, pepper spice, and oak.

The pepper is present but well integrated.

The oak is noticeable but **never bitter**.

The heat is there, but it is **very well hidden**.

And the bourbon continues to evolve as you taste it, revealing different flavors over time.

The biggest warning is that this bourbon is **dangerously drinkable**.

The combination of high proof, creamy texture, sweetness, hidden heat, and a slightly drying finish makes it very easy to keep drinking.

**Drink with water. Drink slowly. Respect the proof.**

# What I'd Change

There isn't much I would change about A125 itself.

If anything, the only thing to change is the **way you approach the pour**.

This bourbon is so easy to drink that the 125-proof alcohol can sneak up on you.

A little water helps open the bourbon and makes the experience more manageable.

My recommendation:

**Don't let the drinkability fool you.**

This is a 125-proof bourbon wearing a very convincing disguise.

# Comparison Notes

## Larceny Barrel Proof A125 vs. Total Wine Private Barrel B524

I've now had both at essentially the same proof:

|            | **Larceny BP A125**                 | **Larceny BP B524 Private Barrel** |
| ---------- | ----------------------------------- | ---------------------------------- |
| Proof      | 125.0                               | 125.4                              |
| ABV        | 62.5%                               | 62.7%                              |
| Price Paid | **Under $40**                       | **~$70**                           |
| Color      | Rich amber                          | Darker, dark amber                 |
| Texture    | **Creamier**                        | Creamy                             |
| Sweetness  | Cherry, caramel, rock candy         | Cherry, caramel, darker oak        |
| Spice      | Integrated pepper                   | **More pronounced pepper spice**   |
| Oak        | Sweet, non-bitter                   | Darker oak character               |
| Heat       | Very well hidden                    | More noticeable                    |
| Overall    | Creamier, sweeter, highly drinkable | Darker, spicier, more intense      |

The two bottles are remarkably close in proof but have distinct personalities.

**A125 is creamier, sweeter, and more approachable.**

**B524 is darker, more pepper-forward, and more intense.**

What makes A125 particularly impressive is the price.

I paid **under $40** for A125 compared with approximately **$70** for the B524 private barrel.

At that price difference, A125 is an **outstanding value**.

B524 may have more private-barrel character and a darker, spicier profile, but A125 gives me an incredibly satisfying barrel-proof wheated bourbon experience for significantly less money.

For my palate, both are excellent—but if I'm judging **value**, A125 wins easily.
