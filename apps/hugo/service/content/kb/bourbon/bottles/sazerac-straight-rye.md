# About Sazerac Rye

Despite carrying the Sazerac name, this whiskey is produced at Buffalo Trace Distillery in Frankfort, Kentucky.

It represents Buffalo Trace's approachable everyday rye and is commonly referred to by enthusiasts as **"Baby Saz."**

Unlike bourbon, rye whiskey must contain at least 51% rye grain, giving it a profile that's often more herbal, peppery, and earthy than the caramel-forward sweetness found in bourbon.

# Appearance

Bright amber with excellent clarity.

Slightly lighter than many of the bourbons on my shelf.

---

# Nose

Immediately different from bourbon.

I found:

- Fresh herbs
- Mint
- Light dill
- Citrus peel
- Honey
- Vanilla
- Light oak

The herbal character is unmistakably rye.

---

# Palate

This isn't trying to imitate bourbon.

Instead it leans into classic rye characteristics.

Notes I found:

- Fresh herbs
- Black pepper
- Mint
- Rye bread
- Light caramel
- Honey
- Gentle oak

Compared to bourbon, the sweetness sits in the background while spice and herbal notes take center stage.

---

# Finish

Medium finish.

Pepper lingers with gentle herbal notes and a touch of oak.

Clean and easy drinking without excessive heat.

---

# Overall Impression

At first I wasn't sure this profile was for me.

The herbal nature felt unusual coming from a bourbon-heavy collection.

But after spending more time with the bottle, I've grown to appreciate what it's offers.

If I want rye, this is exactly the type of profile I'd expect.

# What I'd Change

I'd enjoy a bit more proof.

A Bottled-in-Bond or barrel-proof version would likely add more depth and mouthfeel.

# Final Thoughts

One thing I've learned throughout this whiskey journey is that refining my palate isn't just about finding better bourbon.

It's about learning to appreciate different whiskey styles for what they are.

Sazerac Rye helped me do exactly that.

While I'll probably always reach for bourbon first, I've come to appreciate the herbal spice, mint, and pepper that define a quality rye.

It's approachable, affordable, and serves as an excellent benchmark for the style.
