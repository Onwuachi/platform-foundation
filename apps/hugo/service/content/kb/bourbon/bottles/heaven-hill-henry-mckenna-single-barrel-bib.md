# Why I Bought It

After discovering how much I enjoy Heaven Hill's Bottled-in-Bond bourbons, Henry McKenna Single Barrel naturally became one of the bottles I wanted to experience.

Its reputation had grown significantly after winning major awards, but I wanted to judge it on its own merits rather than the hype.

As one of the few readily available **10-year Bottled-in-Bond single barrel bourbons**, it represents a unique combination of age, proof, and value.

# Appearance

A rich golden amber with excellent clarity.

Long legs slowly coat the glass, suggesting a mature whiskey with good viscosity.

---

# Nose

Classic Heaven Hill immediately comes through.

Notes include:

- Vanilla
- Caramel
- Honey
- Toasted oak
- Baking spice
- Light herbal notes
- Roasted peanuts

The oak is noticeable without becoming overly tannic.

---

# Palate

This is where Henry McKenna shines.

The profile opens with caramel and vanilla before moving into toasted oak, honey, cinnamon, and baking spices.

Unlike younger Bottled-in-Bond bourbons, the additional years in the barrel create a smoother, more refined experience while still maintaining enough spice to stay interesting.

Every barrel is different, but this bottle delivered excellent balance.

---

# Finish

Long and warming.

Oak, cinnamon, caramel, and gentle sweetness linger well after the sip.

The finish never feels overly hot despite the 100 proof.

---

# What I Liked

- Excellent balance
- Mature oak without bitterness
- Rich caramel and vanilla
- Classic Heaven Hill profile
- 10-year age statement
- Bottled-in-Bond authenticity
- Single barrel uniqueness

---

# What I'd Change

Very little.

Because it's a single barrel, consistency can vary between bottles.

The only downside today is availability and pricing, which have increased considerably since it received national recognition.

---

# Food Pairings

# Where It Fits

For me, Henry McKenna sits comfortably among my favorite Heaven Hill releases.

Compared to:

- Heaven Hill 7 Year Bottled-in-Bond
- Elijah Craig Small Batch
- Elijah Craig Barrel Proof

Henry McKenna offers more maturity and elegance while remaining approachable at 100 proof.

If Heaven Hill 7 Year is my everyday pour, Henry McKenna feels like the special occasion version.

---

# Final Thoughts

Henry McKenna reminds me why I enjoy Heaven Hill's style so much.

It isn't trying to overwhelm you with proof or barrel influence.

Instead, it showcases what patient aging can do to a classic bourbon recipe.

The combination of a 10-year age statement, Bottled-in-Bond designation, and single barrel character creates a bourbon that's both approachable and rewarding.

For me, it earns its place as one of the strongest Bottled-in-Bond bourbons currently available.
