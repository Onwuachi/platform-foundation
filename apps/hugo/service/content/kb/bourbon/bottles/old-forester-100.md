# Why I Bought It

Old Forester has become one of the distilleries I consistently enjoy.

After discovering how much I liked Old Forester 1910 and especially 1920, I wanted to revisit their flagship 100-proof bourbon.

Sometimes it's easy to overlook the everyday bottles while chasing premium releases.

Old Forester 100 reminded me that great bourbon doesn't have to be expensive.

# Appearance

Medium amber with bright copper highlights.

Darker than many entry-level bourbons while maintaining excellent clarity.

Good legs suggest a respectable mouthfeel for the proof.

---

# Nose

Classic Brown-Forman.

Immediately recognizable.

Notes include:

- Caramel
- Brown sugar
- Vanilla
- Toasted oak
- Banana Foster
- Baking spices
- Light cinnamon
- Orange peel

The nose is sweet but balanced by oak.

---

# Palate

This is where Old Forester separates itself from many everyday bourbons.

Rich caramel arrives first followed by:

- Brown sugar
- Vanilla
- Toasted oak
- Milk chocolate
- Banana
- Cinnamon
- Light cherry
- Black pepper

Everything feels integrated.

The oak provides structure without becoming bitter.

The sweetness never becomes syrupy.

---

# Finish

Medium-long.

Oak and baking spices gradually replace the caramel sweetness.

Black pepper lingers nicely while the Brown-Forman banana note slowly fades.

Very satisfying for a 100-proof bourbon.

---

# Overall Impression

Old Forester 100 punches well above its price point.

It delivers nearly everything I enjoy from the Brown-Forman profile while remaining affordable enough to drink regularly.

It isn't as rich as 1910.

It isn't as bold as 1920.

Instead, it sits comfortably between them as an excellent everyday bourbon.

---

# What I Liked

- Excellent value
- Rich caramel
- Balanced sweetness
- Good oak influence
- Familiar Brown-Forman character

- Easy to recommend

---

# What I'd Change

Very little.

A touch more viscosity would elevate it even further, but at this price there's little to criticize.

---

# Comparison Notes

## Compared to Wild Turkey 101

These are two of the best everyday bourbons on the market, but they have very different personalities.

### Old Forester 100

- Sweeter profile
- More caramel
- More banana
- Softer oak
- Dessert-like character
- Brown-Forman signature profile

### Wild Turkey 101

- More spice
- More pepper
- More oak
- Higher rye influence
- Alligator-char barrel character
- Better introduction to the Wild Turkey / Russell's profile

Both are outstanding values.

Choosing between them comes down to whether you're craving sweetness or spice.

# Final Thoughts

Old Forester 100 proves why Brown-Forman continues to be one of my favorite bourbon producers.

It delivers classic bourbon flavors with enough sweetness, oak, and spice to stay interesting while remaining incredibly approachable.

As my palate has evolved, I've come to appreciate bottles like this even more.

They don't rely on rarity or hype.

They simply deliver consistently good bourbon every time I pour a glass.
