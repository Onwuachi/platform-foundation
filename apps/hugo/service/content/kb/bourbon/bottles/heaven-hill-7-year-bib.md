# About the Whiskey

Heaven Hill Bottled in Bond is one of the best examples of what the Bottled in Bond Act was designed to protect.

To carry the designation, the bourbon must:

- Be distilled in one season
- By one distiller
- At one distillery
- Matured at least four years in a federally bonded warehouse
- Bottled at exactly 100 proof

Heaven Hill goes even further by aging this expression for **7 years**, providing additional maturity while maintaining remarkable balance.

Unlike many modern premium releases that chase higher proof or extreme oak extraction, this bourbon succeeds through restraint and consistency.

# Palate

This is where Heaven Hill separates itself.

Everything arrives in layers instead of all at once.

That transitions into vanilla and toasted oak before the spice slowly builds.

Flavor notes include:

- Rich caramel
- Brown sugar

- Toasted oak
- Honey
- Cinnamon
- Nutmeg
- Roasted peanuts
- Light cherry
- Orange peel

The sweetness never becomes syrupy.

# Finish

Medium to long.

The caramel gradually fades into toasted oak before black pepper and baking spices linger pleasantly.

The finish remains warm without becoming hot.

Even after several pours, it continues inviting another sip.

---

# Mouthfeel

One of the strengths of this bottle.

Medium-full body with a creamy texture that coats the palate beautifully.

The 100 proof provides enough weight to feel substantial while remaining approachable.

---

# Overall Impression

## Compared to Old Forester 1920

1920 offers darker chocolate, cherry syrup, and significantly more proof.

---

## Compared to Woodford Double Oaked

Woodford emphasizes toasted oak, dessert sweetness, and richness.

Heaven Hill presents a more classic bourbon profile.

---

## Compared to Bank Street Reserve

Bank Street introduces more floral notes and slightly bitter oak.

Heaven Hill feels deeper, smoother, and more complete.

## Compared to Mellow Corn
