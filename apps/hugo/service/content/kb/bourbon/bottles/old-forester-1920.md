# About 1920

Old Forester 1920 is part of the Whiskey Row Series and pays tribute to the Prohibition era, when Old Forester was one of the few distilleries licensed to produce medicinal whiskey.

Bottled at 115 proof, it reflects the robust style of whiskey that was commonly sold during that period.

Unlike many high-proof bourbons that rely on sheer intensity, 1920 maintains exceptional balance while delivering remarkable depth.

These bottles share the same DNA but express it differently.

### 1910

- Relaxing evening pour

### 1920

- Darker caramel
- Bigger oak
- More spice
- Cherry
- Longer finish
- Richer mouthfeel

1910 is comfort.

# Final Thoughts

Old Forester 1920 isn't just another high-proof bourbon.

It's one of the best examples of what modern Kentucky bourbon can be.

The proof isn't there for bragging rights.

It's there to intensify the caramel, deepen the oak, lengthen the finish, and create one of the richest drinking experiences available without venturing into barrel-proof territory.

After tasting dozens of bourbons, this bottle remains one of the clearest expressions of my evolving palate.

It rewards slow sipping, reveals new layers over time, and consistently reminds me why it has earned a permanent place in my Top 5.
