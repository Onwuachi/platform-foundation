# Why I Bought It

As my bourbon journey evolved, I realized I wanted to understand how mash bills influence flavor.

Maker's Mark Cask Strength gave me an opportunity to experience a barrel-proof wheated bourbon without spending allocated-bottle money.

I wanted to see whether wheat could deliver the richness I normally look for in oak-forward bourbons.

# About Maker's Mark Cask Strength

Unlike standard Maker's Mark, this expression is bottled at barrel proof with minimal dilution.

The wheated mash bill replaces rye with red winter wheat, producing a softer and sweeter bourbon profile.

The higher proof amplifies richness while maintaining the smooth character Maker's Mark is known for.

---

# Appearance

Deep amber with rich copper tones.

Excellent legs cling to the glass, hinting at the full-bodied mouthfeel to come.

---

# Nose

Sweet and inviting.

Notes include:

- Caramel
- Vanilla
- Brown sugar
- Honey
- Toffee
- Fresh baked bread
- Toasted oak
- Cinnamon

The ethanol is present but well integrated for the proof.

---

# Palate

Rich, creamy, and full-bodied.

Flavors include:

- Caramel
- Vanilla
- Brown sugar
- Honey
- Toffee
- Baking spices
- Toasted oak
- Light dark fruit

The wheat creates a softer texture than most barrel-proof bourbons.

Rather than delivering aggressive spice, it emphasizes sweetness and richness.

---

# Finish

Medium-long.

Oak, caramel, vanilla, and baking spices linger with gentle warmth.

The finish remains smooth without becoming overly dry.

---

# Overall Impression

Maker's Mark Cask Strength demonstrates just how enjoyable a wheated bourbon can be when bottled at full proof.

It doesn't rely on heavy spice or tannic oak.

Instead, it focuses on sweetness, balance, and a luxurious mouthfeel.

---

# What I Liked

- Rich caramel
- Thick mouthfeel
- Excellent proof integration
- Creamy texture
- Brown sugar sweetness
- Great value

---

# What I'd Change

I'd enjoy a little more mature oak character.

While the sweetness is excellent, I generally prefer bourbons that lean slightly further toward toasted oak and dark fruit.

---

# Comparison Notes

## Compared with Wild Turkey Rare Breed

**Rare Breed**

- More oak
- More spice
- Longer finish
- Greater complexity

**Maker's Mark Cask Strength**

- Sweeter
- Creamier
- Softer
- More dessert-like
- Less rye influence

Compared with Larceny Barrel Proof, Maker's Mark Cask Strength feels more polished and balanced, while Larceny often delivers bigger oak and intensity.

## ✅ Absolutely.

---

# Final Thoughts

Maker's Mark Cask Strength changed my opinion of wheated bourbon.

Rather than feeling soft or lacking character, it delivers tremendous richness while maintaining remarkable balance.

Although I still gravitate toward oak-forward bottles like Wild Turkey Rare Breed, Russell's Reserve Single Barrel, and Old Forester 1920, this bottle has earned a permanent place on my shelf as one of the best examples of the wheated style.

---

**9.0 / 10**
