# Why I Bought It

Costco has built a surprisingly strong reputation for its private-label spirits, and their Bottled-in-Bond bourbon quickly became one of the bottles I wanted to evaluate for myself.

Rather than judging it on hype alone, I wanted to compare it directly against Evan Williams Bottled-in-Bond—my longtime benchmark for affordable Bottled-in-Bond bourbon.

At **$24.99 for a one-liter bottle**, it sits in an interesting position: more expensive than Evan Williams but still firmly in the value category.

# Appearance

A rich amber with excellent clarity.

Long, slow legs suggest a surprisingly full-bodied bourbon.

It certainly doesn't look like a budget bottle.

---

# Nose

The nose is softer and more refined than I expected.

I immediately picked up:

- Honey
- Vanilla
- Light caramel
- Candied fruit
- Gentle oak
- Baking spice

Everything feels well integrated.

Unlike some younger bourbons, there isn't a sharp ethanol note competing with the aromas.

---

# Palate

The first thing I noticed was how approachable this bourbon is.

Honey, vanilla, caramel, and light oak arrive together before giving way to gentle baking spice.

Compared with Evan Williams Bottled-in-Bond, Kirkland leans noticeably more floral and refined.

It carries less pepper and less oak, replacing those with a smoother, sweeter profile.

The balance is excellent.

Nothing dominates the palate.

Instead, everything feels cohesive from beginning to end.

---

# Finish

Medium in length.

Sweet oak, vanilla, and gentle baking spice linger while the proof remains remarkably controlled.

The finish stays soft and approachable without becoming overly sweet.

---

# Overall Impression

Kirkland Signature Bottled-in-Bond exceeded my expectations.

Instead of simply being a budget bourbon wearing a private-label badge, it delivers a genuinely polished drinking experience.

It's smoother than many Bottled-in-Bond bourbons while still maintaining enough oak and spice to remain interesting.

---

# What I Liked

- Excellent balance
- Smooth for 100 proof
- Integrated flavors
- Pleasant floral character
- Honey and vanilla sweetness
- Outstanding value
- Easy sipping

---

# What I'd Change

I'd like just a little more oak and a touch more spice.

The softer profile makes it extremely approachable, but I occasionally find myself wanting just a little more Kentucky character.

---

# Comparison Notes

Compared with Evan Williams Bottled-in-Bond:

- Smoother
- More floral
- Less pepper
- Less oak
- Slightly sweeter
- More refined

Compared with Heaven Hill Bottled-in-Bond:

- Softer overall
- Less oak influence
- Less complexity
- Easier to drink

Compared with Bardstown Bottled-in-Bond:

- Similar balance
- Less mature
- Slightly lighter body

If Evan Williams is the rugged Kentucky workhorse, Kirkland is the polished everyday sipper.

# Shelf Role

**Value Bottle**

A smooth, easy-drinking Bottled-in-Bond that works equally well for casual sipping or introducing someone to 100-proof bourbon.

---

# Buy Again?

Absolutely.

While Evan Williams remains my favorite everyday Bottled-in-Bond, Kirkland offers enough refinement and balance that I'll happily keep a bottle on the shelf whenever Costco has it available.

---

# Final Thoughts

Kirkland Signature Bottled-in-Bond surprised me.

I expected a decent value bourbon.

Instead, I found a polished, well-balanced whiskey that confidently stands on its own.

Would I choose it over Evan Williams?

Probably not.

I prefer the extra oak, spice, and classic Kentucky character that Evan Williams delivers.

But that doesn't diminish what Kirkland accomplishes.

It's simply a different expression of Bottled-in-Bond bourbon—one that favors balance and refinement over boldness.

For anyone looking for a smooth 100-proof bourbon at a reasonable price, this is an easy recommendation.

---

# Rating

**8.7 / 10**

A refined, approachable Bottled-in-Bond bourbon with excellent balance, surprising polish, and outstanding value. While I still slightly prefer Evan Williams for its bolder Kentucky character, Kirkland earns its place as one of the better values in the Bottled-in-Bond category.
