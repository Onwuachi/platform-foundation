# About This Bourbon

Bardstown Bottled in Bond is produced entirely under the requirements of the Bottled-in-Bond Act of 1897.

Unlike many early Bardstown releases that featured sourced whiskey, this expression showcases the company's own distillate. It reflects Bardstown's goal of blending traditional bourbon standards with a modern production philosophy.

---

# Appearance

Medium amber with bright copper highlights.

Clean and inviting in the glass, with slow-moving legs that suggest a balanced, medium-bodied bourbon.

---

# Nose

Classic Kentucky bourbon.

Notes include:

- Caramel
- Vanilla
- Brown sugar
- Toasted oak
- Honey
- Baking spices
- Light orange zest

The aroma is balanced and approachable without excessive ethanol.

---

# Palate

Traditional and easy to enjoy.

Flavors include:

- Caramel
- Brown sugar
- Vanilla
- Toasted oak
- Cinnamon
- Light pepper
- Roasted nuts
- Honey

Everything feels well integrated, with no single note overpowering the others.

---

# Finish

Medium length.

Oak and baking spices linger alongside caramel sweetness before fading into gentle pepper.

Clean, pleasant, and balanced.

---

# Overall Impression

Bardstown Bottled in Bond is a very well-made bourbon that emphasizes balance over intensity.

Nothing feels out of place, and every sip reinforces the impression of a thoughtfully crafted Bottled-in-Bond whiskey.

While it doesn't quite deliver the depth or richness of my favorite BiB bottles, it's consistently enjoyable.

---

# What I Liked

- Classic bourbon profile
- Well-balanced sweetness and spice
- Pleasant oak
- Clean finish
- Excellent proof integration
- Easy sipping

---

# What I'd Change

I'd like a little more barrel influence and a richer mouthfeel.

Compared to some of my favorites, it feels slightly restrained.

# Comparison Notes

## Compared with Heaven Hill Bottled in Bond

**Heaven Hill BiB**

- Richer oak
- Bigger caramel
- More developed finish
- Better overall value
- Greater depth

**Bardstown BiB**

- Slightly brighter
- More delicate
- Cleaner profile
- Softer spice
- Very approachable

Heaven Hill remains my benchmark for Bottled-in-Bond bourbon, but Bardstown offers a polished and modern interpretation that is enjoyable in its own right.

# Buy Again?

## ✅ Yes

While it doesn't replace Heaven Hill Bottled in Bond on my shelf, it's a bottle I'd happily own again and recommend to anyone looking to explore modern Bottled-in-Bond bourbons.

---

# Final Thoughts

Bardstown Bottled in Bond proves that Bardstown Bourbon Company can produce an impressive Bottled-in-Bond bourbon using its own whiskey.

It's balanced, approachable, and well-crafted, delivering classic caramel, vanilla, toasted oak, and baking spice without trying to overwhelm the palate.

For my personal preferences, I still gravitate toward bourbons with deeper oak, richer caramel, and a longer finish, which keeps Heaven Hill Bottled in Bond as my benchmark. Even so, Bardstown BiB has earned my respect as a dependable, modern take on one of bourbon's most historic designations.
