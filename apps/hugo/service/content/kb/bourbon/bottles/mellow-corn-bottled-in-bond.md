# About Corn Whiskey

Although it's often found beside bourbon on store shelves, **Mellow Corn is not a bourbon**.

Instead, it's a **Straight Corn Whiskey**.

Unlike bourbon, which must contain at least 51% corn and be aged in **new charred oak barrels**, corn whiskey must contain **at least 80% corn** and is commonly matured in either used oak barrels or uncharred new oak.

The result is a whiskey that emphasizes the sweetness of the grain itself rather than heavy oak influence.

Mellow Corn is also one of the few remaining Bottled-in-Bond corn whiskeys still produced today, making it something of a living piece of American whiskey history.

# Appearance

Immediately lighter than almost every bourbon on my shelf.

Rather than a deep amber, the color leans toward a bright golden yellow with excellent clarity.

The lighter color hints that this whiskey relies less on heavy barrel influence and more on the character of the corn itself.

---

# Nose

The nose surprised me.

It's light, clean, and inviting without feeling overly sweet.

Notes I found included:

- Light floral notes
- Caramel
- Toffee
- Brown sugar
- Sweet corn
- Vanilla
- Gentle grain sweetness

The aroma feels fresh and approachable rather than oak-driven.

---

# Palate

This is where Mellow Corn really won me over.

It's balanced from beginning to end.

The sweetness comes first but never becomes syrupy.

I found:

- Creamed corn
- Caramel
- Toffee
- Brown sugar
- Vanilla
- Honey
- Light baking spice
- Gentle black pepper

Despite being **100 proof**, it drinks remarkably easy.

Everything feels integrated, making it one of the most approachable Bottled-in-Bond whiskeys I've tasted.

---

# Finish

Medium length.

The sweetness slowly gives way to gentle spice with a touch of pepper lingering on the tongue.

A light oak note appears near the end, but the finish remains crisp, clean, and surprisingly elegant.

---

# Overall Impression

This bottle completely exceeded my expectations.

At no point did it feel like I was drinking a "budget whiskey."

Instead, it felt like I was drinking a whiskey that simply prioritizes balance over intensity.

There's enough sweetness, enough spice, and enough structure to remain interesting while never becoming aggressive.

# What I'd Change

Very little.

As it stands, the proof feels exceptionally well chosen for the style.

# Comparison Notes

The closest comparison in my collection is **Heaven Hill Bottled-in-Bond**, though the two whiskies serve very different purposes.

Compared with Heaven Hill BiB:

**Heaven Hill BiB**

- More oak
- More baking spice
- Darker caramel
- More traditional bourbon profile

**Mellow Corn**

- Sweeter grain character
- More floral
- Creamier mouthfeel
- Less oak influence
- Softer overall profile

Rather than competing with bourbon, Mellow Corn showcases what corn whiskey can be when it's produced with care.
