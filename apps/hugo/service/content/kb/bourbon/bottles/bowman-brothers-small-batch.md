# Why I Bought It

Bowman Brothers Small Batch intrigued me because of its unusual history.

Although it's produced in Virginia, much of its story begins in Kentucky. The bourbon starts as distillate produced by Buffalo Trace before being shipped to A. Smith Bowman Distillery in Fredericksburg, Virginia. Bowman then redistills, matures, blends, and bottles the whiskey, creating a bourbon that develops its own distinct personality.

I wanted to answer a simple question:

Can the same starting distillate become something noticeably different through another distillery's process?

# About the Distillery

One of the most interesting aspects of Bowman Brothers is its production story.

A. Smith Bowman Distillery is located in **Fredericksburg, Virginia**, making it one of the few significant bourbon producers outside Kentucky.

The whiskey is widely believed to begin as Buffalo Trace distillate produced in Kentucky. After arriving in Virginia, Bowman performs an additional distillation before aging, blending, and bottling the bourbon.

Because of this additional processing and maturation, Bowman Brothers is **not simply Buffalo Trace in a different bottle**.

It develops its own identity while still sharing some of the approachable characteristics that make Buffalo Trace bourbons popular.

The aroma is inviting and approachable.

Notes include:

- Caramel
- Vanilla
- Honey
- Orange zest
- Light floral notes
- Sweet oak

Compared with many Kentucky bourbons, the nose feels softer and brighter.

---

# Palate

Balanced and easy drinking.

I found:

- Caramel
- Vanilla
- Honey
- Brown sugar
- Light baking spice
- Orange citrus
- Gentle oak

The sweetness remains well controlled without becoming syrupy.

---

# Finish

Medium length.

The finish brings gentle oak and light pepper alongside lingering caramel sweetness.

It fades cleanly without excessive heat.

---

# Overall Impression

Bowman Brothers succeeds because of its balance.

Nothing feels aggressive.

Instead, it offers an approachable bourbon with enough complexity to remain interesting while staying incredibly easy to drink.

# What I'd Change

At 90 proof, I found myself wondering what this profile would taste like at Bottled-in-Bond or barrel strength.

A little more proof could add richness and increase the depth of the finish.

---

# Comparison Notes

This bottle naturally invites comparison to Buffalo Trace because of its production history.

While they likely begin from similar distillate, Bowman Brothers develops into something different.

Compared to Buffalo Trace:

- Slightly softer
- More floral
- More citrus
- Less cherry-driven
- Gentler finish

Compared to Heaven Hill Bottled-in-Bond:

- Less oak
- Less richness
- More delicate sweetness
- Lower proof makes it easier to sip

Rather than replacing either bottle, Bowman occupies its own space as an approachable, balanced everyday bourbon.

# Final Thoughts

Bowman Brothers Small Batch demonstrates that bourbon's identity isn't determined solely by where the distillate begins.

The additional work performed by A. Smith Bowman creates a whiskey that stands comfortably on its own merits.

While bourbon enthusiasts often focus on mash bills or age statements, Bowman reminds us that distillation, maturation, and blending all contribute to a bourbon's final character.

It's approachable, well balanced, and offers an interesting chapter in the broader story of American bourbon.

# Rating

**8.6 / 10**

A balanced Virginia bourbon with an unusually interesting origin story. Its connection to Buffalo Trace makes it fascinating, but its own craftsmanship is what makes it worth drinking.
