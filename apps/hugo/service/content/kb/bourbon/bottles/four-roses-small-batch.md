# About Four Roses Small Batch

Unlike many bourbons built around a single mash bill, Four Roses produces ten unique bourbon recipes by combining two mash bills with five proprietary yeast strains.

Small Batch is created by blending four of those recipes, producing a bourbon known for balance rather than intensity.

Its reputation has long been that of an approachable bourbon emphasizing fruit, sweetness, and elegance over heavy oak.

---

# Appearance

Medium amber.

Slightly lighter than many of my preferred higher-proof bourbons with good clarity and moderate legs.

---

# Nose

Very inviting.

The nose leans toward bright fruit instead of heavy barrel influence.

I found:

- Honey
- Caramel
- Red berries
- Light cherry
- Floral notes
- Vanilla
- Gentle oak

The aroma is clean and approachable without much ethanol.

---

# Palate

This is a very balanced bourbon.

Nothing dominates.

Notes I found included:

- Caramel
- Vanilla
- Honey
- Red fruit
- Light cherry
- Baking spice
- Gentle oak
- Mild pepper

The flavors arrive softly and remain balanced throughout.

Compared to many of my favorites, it doesn't deliver the same richness, viscosity, or oak depth.

---

# Finish

Medium length.

Light pepper and baking spice linger alongside vanilla and soft oak.

The finish is pleasant but fades sooner than higher-proof bottles like Rare Breed or Old Forester 1920.

---

# Overall Impression

Four Roses Small Batch is an easy bourbon to recommend.

It's approachable, balanced, and very well made.

As my palate has evolved, however, I've realized I generally gravitate toward bourbons with more oak, richer caramel, darker fruit, and higher proof.

This bottle doesn't do anything wrong—it simply isn't trying to be that style of bourbon.

---

# What I Liked

- Extremely balanced
- Fruity profile
- Easy sipping
- Little ethanol
- Great introduction to Four Roses
- Excellent value

---

# What I'd Change

I'd like more:

- Oak
- Rich caramel
- Mouthfeel
- Finish
- Proof

Those characteristics are what usually elevate a bourbon into my personal Top 5.

# Buy Again?

## ✅ Yes

---

# Final Thoughts

Four Roses Small Batch helped me understand something about my own palate.

While I appreciate its balance, fruit-forward profile, and drinkability, I consistently find myself reaching for bourbons with more barrel influence and higher proof.

That doesn't make Four Roses Small Batch worse—it simply means it targets a different style of bourbon drinker.

If someone asked me for a smooth, balanced, easy-drinking bourbon under $40, this would remain one of my recommendations.

---

# Rating

**8.3 / 10**

A well-crafted, approachable bourbon with excellent balance and fruit-forward character. While it no longer aligns perfectly with my evolving preference for richer oak-forward, higher-proof pours, it's still an easy bottle to recommend.
