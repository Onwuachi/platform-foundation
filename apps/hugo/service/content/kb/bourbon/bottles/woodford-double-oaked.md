# About Double Oaking

Unlike a traditional bourbon that matures in a single new charred oak barrel, Woodford Reserve Double Oaked is transferred into a second heavily toasted, lightly charred barrel for additional maturation.

Rather than simply increasing oak, the second barrel develops deeper caramelization and layered dessert flavors.

The result is one of the richest standard-shelf bourbons available.

---

# Appearance

Deep amber with rich copper highlights.

The extra barrel maturation gives it a darker appearance than standard Woodford Reserve.

Slow, oily legs hint at a rich mouthfeel.

---

# Nose

The aroma is immediately recognizable.

I found:

- Toasted oak
- Caramel
- Milk chocolate
- Marshmallow
- Brown sugar
- Vanilla
- Maple syrup
- Toasted pecans
- Cinnamon

It smells more like a dessert than a traditional bourbon.

---

# Palate

This is exactly why the bottle stays in my Top Five.

Rich without becoming syrupy.

Notes include:

- Dark caramel
- Brown sugar
- Chocolate
- Toasted marshmallow
- Vanilla
- Maple
- Toffee
- Toasted oak
- Roasted nuts

The sweetness is balanced beautifully by toasted oak.

Everything feels layered and incredibly smooth.

---

# Finish

Long and warming.

Chocolate, toasted oak, vanilla, and caramel continue well after the sip.

The finish isn't dominated by spice.

Instead, it slowly fades into rich toasted sweetness.

---

# Overall Impression

Woodford Reserve Double Oaked is one of the most unique bottles on my shelf.

Rather than chasing proof or spice, it focuses entirely on richness and barrel character.

It isn't trying to overpower you.

It's trying to showcase what carefully managed oak can accomplish.

---

- Incredible dessert profile

# What I'd Change

Very little.

Occasionally I wonder what this profile would taste like around 100–110 proof, but even at 90.4 proof it delivers remarkable richness.

# Comparison Notes

### Compared with Old Forester 1910

- Similar Brown-Forman DNA
- Both feature secondary barrel maturation
- 1910 is fruitier
- Woodford is creamier
- Woodford emphasizes chocolate and marshmallow
- 1910 offers a little more spice

### Compared with Bondstone Double Oak Cask Strength

- Woodford is more refined
- Woodford is sweeter
- Bondstone is hotter
- Bondstone emphasizes oak over dessert flavors

### Compared with Rare Breed

These bottles serve completely different purposes.

Rare Breed is my everyday premium pour.

Woodford Double Oaked is my evening dessert pour.

# Final Thoughts

Woodford Reserve Double Oaked helped me realize just how much I enjoy well-developed barrel influence.

Rich oak, chocolate, caramel, toasted marshmallow, and an incredibly silky mouthfeel combine to create a bourbon unlike almost anything else on the shelf.

It may not be the highest-proof bourbon in my collection, but proof isn't the point here.

The craftsmanship lies in how the second barrel transforms an already excellent bourbon into something richer, sweeter, and remarkably elegant.

It's one of the bottles that continues to define my palate.

---

# Rating

**9.3 / 10**

A benchmark double-oaked bourbon that delivers one of the richest dessert-style experiences available without sacrificing balance or drinkability.
