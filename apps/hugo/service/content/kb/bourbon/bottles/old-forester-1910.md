# Why I Bought It

Old Forester 1910 was one of the first bourbons that completely changed how I thought about oak.

Instead of chasing higher proof, this bottle showed me how a second barrel can transform a bourbon into something rich, silky, and dessert-like without becoming overly oaked.

It became one of the bottles that helped shape my palate and eventually led me toward other favorites like Woodford Reserve Double Oaked.

# About 1910

Old Forester 1910 is part of the **Whiskey Row Series**.

Its name commemorates the 1910 fire at Old Forester's distillery, when whiskey was moved into heavily charred barrels while the facility was repaired. Inspired by that event, today's 1910 is finished in a second heavily charred barrel, creating a richer and sweeter profile.

While it isn't labeled a "double oaked bourbon," that's effectively what it is.

---

# Appearance

Deep mahogany with dark copper highlights.

Noticeably darker than standard Old Forester expressions thanks to its second barrel maturation.

Slow, oily legs hint at the rich mouthfeel to come.

---

# Nose

An incredibly inviting nose.

Rich and dessert-like without becoming overly sweet.

Notes include:

- Dark caramel
- Toffee
- Milk chocolate
- Toasted marshmallow
- Brown sugar
- Vanilla bean
- Toasted oak
- Banana Foster
- Light cherry

Everything feels warm, rich, and well integrated.

---

# Palate

This is where 1910 shines.

The mouthfeel is rich and almost creamy.

Flavors unfold in layers:

- Dark caramel
- Toffee
- Chocolate fudge
- Brown sugar
- Vanilla
- Toasted oak
- Roasted marshmallow
- Banana Foster
- Cinnamon
- Light baking spices

The lower proof allows the sweetness and oak to take center stage.

Rather than overwhelming the palate, every flavor complements the next.

---

# Finish

Medium to long.

The sweetness gradually gives way to toasted oak, light cocoa, and gentle baking spices.

A soft warmth lingers without ever becoming hot.

It's the kind of finish that invites another sip.

---

# Overall Impression

Old Forester 1910 is one of the best examples of a modern dessert bourbon.

Everything feels intentional.

The second barrel doesn't simply add oak—it adds richness, texture, and complexity while maintaining remarkable balance.

Whenever I'm in the mood for something relaxing and decadent, this bottle rarely disappoints.

---

# What I Liked

- Exceptional mouthfeel

# What I'd Change

Very little.

Occasionally I wish for a barrel-proof version with this same profile, but part of 1910's charm is how approachable it is at 93 proof.

# Comparison Notes

## Compared to Woodford Reserve Double Oaked

These two bottles occupy similar territory, but each has its own personality.

### Old Forester 1910

- More caramel
- More banana
- Softer oak
- More chocolate
- Sweeter overall
- Creamier mouthfeel

### Woodford Reserve Double Oaked

- More toasted oak
- More espresso
- More dark chocolate
- Slightly drier finish
- More barrel-forward
- Greater oak intensity

If I'm craving dessert sweetness, I often reach for 1910.

If I want deeper toasted oak, Woodford Double Oaked usually gets the nod.

---

## Compared to Old Forester 1920

1910 and 1920 are complementary bottles.

1910 emphasizes richness and sweetness.

1920 emphasizes power, proof, and complexity.

Together they represent two outstanding expressions of the Brown-Forman profile.

# Final Thoughts

Old Forester 1910 taught me that proof isn't everything.

Sometimes balance, texture, and thoughtful barrel finishing create an experience that's every bit as memorable as a barrel-proof bourbon.

While Old Forester 1920 ultimately ranks higher in my personal Top 5 because of its boldness and complexity, 1910 remains one of the most enjoyable pours on my shelf.

It represents everything I appreciate about well-executed secondary maturation: rich caramel, toasted oak, chocolate, and a silky mouthfeel that keeps me coming back.
