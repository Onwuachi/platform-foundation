# Why I Bought It

I've become very familiar with the Knob Creek lineup through Knob Creek 9 Year, Knob Creek 12 Year, and Knob Creek Smoked Maple.

When Blender's Edition 01 was released, I was interested in seeing how Beam's blending team could refine the familiar Knob Creek profile without relying on additional age statements or barrel finishes.

Rather than simply offering another single barrel, this release promised a deliberately crafted blend intended to showcase balance and complexity.

# Appearance

Rich amber with excellent clarity.

The whiskey develops slow, oily legs that hint at a full-bodied pour.

---

# Nose

The nose is immediately recognizable as Knob Creek, but noticeably more refined.

I found:

- Rich caramel
- Vanilla
- Roasted peanuts
- Toasted oak
- Brown sugar
- Baking spice

The oak feels mature without becoming overly tannic, and everything comes across as well integrated.

---

# Palate

This is where Blender's Edition separates itself from the standard Knob Creek offerings.

The palate is richer, rounder, and more cohesive.

Notes included:

- Caramel
- Toffee
- Vanilla
- Roasted peanuts
- Brown sugar
- Toasted oak
- Baking spice
- Light dark fruit

Nothing dominates.

Instead, each flavor builds on the next, creating a profile that feels intentionally composed rather than simply barrel-driven.

---

# Finish

Medium-long.

Oak and baking spice linger alongside warm caramel sweetness.

The finish stays balanced without becoming dry or overly peppery.

---

# Overall Impression

Blender's Edition 01 demonstrates what careful blending can accomplish.

Rather than chasing higher proof or extreme oak influence, Beam focused on harmony and integration.

The result is one of the most polished expressions in the Knob Creek lineup.

---

# What I Liked

- Excellent balance
- Rich mouthfeel
- Well-integrated oak
- Refined Beam peanut profile
- Consistent complexity from nose through finish
- Easy to sip while remaining flavorful

---

# What I'd Change

Very little.

If I could change one thing, I'd be interested in seeing this same blend released at barrel proof to explore how additional proof would impact the richness and finish.

---

# Comparison Notes

Within the Knob Creek lineup, I'd place it above the standard 9 Year and alongside the excellent 12 Year, although each offers a different experience.

Compared to:

- **Knob Creek 9 Year** — noticeably more refined and balanced.
- **Knob Creek 12 Year** — less oak-driven but equally enjoyable in its own way.
- **Knob Creek Smoked Maple** — a completely different purpose, with Blender's Edition emphasizing classic bourbon character.

This is one of the better examples of what Beam can accomplish through blending rather than simply increasing age or proof.

# Buy Again?

✅ Absolutely.

If Beam continues the Blender's Edition series at this level of quality, I'll happily continue exploring future releases.

---

# Final Thoughts

Knob Creek Blender's Edition 01 reinforced something I've come to appreciate about the Beam profile.

When the oak, caramel, peanut notes, and baking spice are brought together with restraint and balance, the result is a bourbon that feels complete rather than aggressive.

It may not have the age statement of Knob Creek 12 or the intensity of a barrel-proof release, but it doesn't need either.  Think of it as Knob 10 or or Knob 10-01)

This bottle succeeds because every element feels intentional.

For me, it's one of the strongest modern Knob Creek releases and one I'd confidently recommend to anyone who enjoys traditional Kentucky bourbon with excellent balance.

---

# Rating

**9.1 / 10**

An exceptionally well-balanced Knob Creek that showcases the value of thoughtful blending. One of the most refined Beam expressions in my collection.
