# Why I Bought It

I wasn't expecting much from a grocery store private label, but the specifications immediately stood out:

- Bottled in Bond
- 100 proof
- Wheated mashbill
- Less than $30
- Seven-year stated age
- Selected and bottled by Cat's Eye Distillery

For the price, it was simply too interesting to pass up.

# Appearance

Rich amber with good legs. Looks older than the price suggests.

---

# Nose

Caramel, vanilla, milk chocolate, toasted oak, brown sugar and ripe cherries.

The oak is immediately noticeable but remains balanced. Very little ethanol despite being bottled at 100 proof.

---

# Palate

Cherry, caramel, chocolate, brown sugar and toasted wheat dominate the palate.

The mouthfeel is medium-bodied with enough oak to remind you this is an older bourbon. A pleasant rye-like spice develops despite the wheated mashbill, keeping the sweetness from becoming overwhelming.

---

# Finish

Medium length.

Sweet toffee transitions into mild oak, leather and a pleasantly drying finish that encourages another sip.

This drying characteristic reminds me of Wild Turkey products, although without the signature Wild Turkey funk.

---

# Overall Impression

This bottle dramatically exceeded my expectations.

At under $30, it delivers mature oak, classic wheated sweetness and enough complexity to keep experienced bourbon drinkers interested.

It isn't as polished or integrated as Heaven Hill Bottled in Bond, but it comes surprisingly close considering the price.

---

# What I Liked

- Outstanding value
- Bottled in Bond
- Mature oak
- Cherry notes
- Excellent proof
- Minimal ethanol
- Very approachable

---

# What I'd Change

A little more integration between the sweeter wheated profile and the oak would elevate it another level.

The finish could linger slightly longer.

---

# Comparison Notes

### Heaven Hill Bottled in Bond

Heaven Hill remains the more refined bourbon with better integration and balance.

### Wild Turkey 101

Shares the slightly drying finish but lacks the signature Wild Turkey funk.

### Maker's Mark

Older, oakier and considerably more complex while maintaining the approachable wheated profile.

# Buy Again?

Absolutely.

This is one of the better value purchases I've made in 2026.

If Hy-Vee continues sourcing barrels of this quality, I'll happily keep one on the shelf.

---

# Final Thoughts

Unrestricted Wheated Bottled in Bond is proof that grocery store private labels don't have to be mediocre.

Between the age, Bottled in Bond designation, mature oak profile and sub-$30 price, this bottle significantly over-delivers.

While Heaven Hill Bottled in Bond remains my benchmark in this category, Unrestricted earns a permanent place as an outstanding value bourbon. I'd recommend it to both newcomers looking for an approachable pour and enthusiasts who appreciate finding hidden gems.
