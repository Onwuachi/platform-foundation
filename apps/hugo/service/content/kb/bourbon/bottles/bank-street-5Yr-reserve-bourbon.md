# Why I Bought It

I originally went to Total Wine looking for a few bottles that had been on my wish list, but they weren't available.

Rather than leave empty-handed, I picked up **Bank Street 5 Year Reserve Bourbon** as another value-oriented bottle to compare against my current benchmark, **Heaven Hill Bottled-in-Bond**.

The goal wasn't to find a unicorn.

It was to answer a simple question:

**Can this become another dependable everyday pour?**

# Appearance

Light amber in color.

Brighter than many of the Heaven Hill and Wild Turkey bottles on my shelf, with moderate legs that suggest a medium-bodied pour.

---

# Nose

The nose immediately stood out.

It is noticeably brighter and more floral than expected.

Notes included:

- Caramel
- Brown sugar
- Oak
- Orange bitters
- Light floral notes

Compared with Heaven Hill Bottled-in-Bond, the aroma feels lighter and more aromatic.

---

# Palate

Easy drinking with a pleasant balance.

The sweetness leans more toward molasses than dark cherry.

Notes I found included:

- Caramel
- Brown sugar
- Molasses
- Oak
- Orange bitters
- Black pepper

The oak carries a slight bitterness that keeps it from reaching the richness of some of my favorite everyday bourbons.

---

# Finish

Medium length.

Black pepper lingers throughout the finish without becoming overwhelming.

The oak remains present alongside a crisp, slightly dry ending.

---

# Overall Impression

This is an enjoyable everyday bourbon.

It doesn't have the depth or richness of Heaven Hill Bottled-in-Bond, but it also doesn't try to be something it isn't.

Instead, it offers a balanced profile that's approachable and easy to sip.

---

# What I Liked

- Bright floral nose
- Balanced sweetness
- Orange bitters added an interesting layer
- Easy drinking
- Nice everyday proof
- Pleasant pepper finish

---

# What I'd Change

The oak develops a slight bitterness that occasionally distracts from the sweeter caramel notes.

A little more richness or barrel influence would help round out the profile.

---

# Comparison Notes

My closest comparison is **Heaven Hill Bottled-in-Bond**.

Both offer solid value and traditional bourbon profiles.

Where Heaven Hill delivers:

- richer oak
- darker caramel
- more integrated sweetness

Bank Street feels:

- brighter
- slightly more floral
- lighter-bodied
- a touch more bitter on the oak

It lands just below Heaven Hill as an everyday sipper but remains a bottle I'd happily pour during the week.

---

# Food Pairings

- Smoked burgers
- Pulled pork

---

# Shelf Role

**Everyday Pour**

A dependable weekday bourbon that drinks easily without demanding attention.

---

# Buy Again?

✅ Yes

If priced competitively, I'd consider buying another bottle.

Not because it beats my favorites, but because it represents solid value.

---

# Final Thoughts

Bank Street 5 Year Reserve doesn't replace Heaven Hill Bottled-in-Bond as my benchmark value bourbon.

What it does offer is another dependable everyday option with its own personality.

The brighter floral nose, hints of orange bitters, and crisp pepper finish give it enough character to stand on its own while remaining approachable and easy to enjoy.

It's a bottle I'm glad I picked up, and one I'll happily continue exploring over the coming weeks.

---

# Rating

**8.2 / 10**

A solid value bourbon with a bright nose, balanced sweetness, and enough character to earn a place as an everyday pour.
