# About Wild Turkey 101

Wild Turkey is distilled under the guidance of bourbon legends Jimmy Russell and Eddie Russell.

The bourbon is matured in barrels with Wild Turkey's signature **No. 4 "alligator" char**, one of the deepest char levels commonly used in Kentucky bourbon.

Although WT101 is chill-filtered, it retains excellent texture and body.

Think of this bottle as the gateway into the Wild Turkey family.

From here, the natural progression is:

- Wild Turkey 101
- Kentucky Spirit
- Rare Breed
- Russell's Reserve 10
- Russell's Reserve Single Barrel

Each step builds on the same familiar Wild Turkey DNA.

# Appearance

Medium to dark amber.

Darker than many entry-level bourbons with good viscosity in the glass.

---

# Nose

Classic Kentucky bourbon.

I found:

- Caramel
- Vanilla
- Toasted oak
- Brown sugar
- Cinnamon
- Orange peel
- Black pepper

The oak is immediately noticeable without overpowering the sweeter aromas.

---

# Palate

This is where WT101 punches well above its price.

Flavors include:

- Rich caramel
- Brown sugar
- Vanilla
- Toasted oak
- Cinnamon
- Baking spice
- Orange zest
- Black pepper

The 101 proof gives it enough intensity to remain interesting while still being approachable.

---

# Finish

Medium-long.

Oak and pepper linger before fading into caramel sweetness.

A satisfying finish that encourages another sip.

---

# Overall Impression

WT101 represents traditional Kentucky bourbon exceptionally well.

Nothing feels exaggerated.

Everything is balanced.

It's approachable for newer drinkers while remaining interesting enough for experienced enthusiasts.

# Comparison Notes

## Compared to Old Forester 100

These are two of the best everyday bourbons available.

**Old Forester 100**

- More cherry
- Sweeter
- Banana notes
- Brown-Forman profile
- Slightly softer spice

**Wild Turkey 101**

- More oak
- More pepper
- More baking spice
- More traditional Kentucky profile
- Better barrel character

I slightly prefer WT101 because of its richer oak profile and more pronounced spice.

---

## Compared to Rare Breed

Rare Breed feels like WT101 turned up to eleven.

- More concentrated
- More viscous
- Longer finish
- Bigger oak
- More caramel
- More complexity

The family resemblance is unmistakable.

# Final Thoughts

Wild Turkey 101 isn't exciting because it's rare.

It's exciting because it gets the fundamentals right.

The signature alligator-char barrels provide rich oak, caramel, vanilla, and baking spice, while the 101 proof delivers enough intensity without sacrificing drinkability.

For anyone wanting to understand the Wild Turkey profile, this is where the journey begins.

After spending time with WT101, it's easy to understand why bottles like Rare Breed and Russell's Reserve Single Barrel have become some of my personal favorites.
