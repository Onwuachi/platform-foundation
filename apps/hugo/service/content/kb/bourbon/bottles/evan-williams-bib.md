# Why I Bought It

After discovering how much I enjoy Heaven Hill's bourbon profile, Evan Williams Bottled-in-Bond quickly became one of my benchmark everyday pours.

At just **$20.99 for a one-liter bottle**, it consistently delivers more flavor, character, and authenticity than many bourbons costing twice as much.

Whenever someone asks me for an affordable bourbon that truly represents Kentucky Bottled-in-Bond whiskey, this is one of the first bottles I recommend.

# Appearance

Medium amber with excellent clarity.

Good legs slowly coat the glass, hinting at a richer mouthfeel than its price would suggest.

---

# Nose

Immediately recognizable as a Heaven Hill bourbon.

Notes include:

- Vanilla
- Caramel
- Toasted oak
- Roasted peanuts
- Baking spice
- Black pepper
- Light citrus

Compared to Kirkland Bottled-in-Bond, the nose is a little rougher around the edges, but that rustic character is exactly what I enjoy.

---

# Palate

This is where Evan Williams earns its reputation.

Caramel and vanilla arrive first before transitioning into toasted oak, roasted peanuts, black pepper, cinnamon, and rye spice.

It drinks like a classic Kentucky bourbon.

Compared to Kirkland Bottled-in-Bond, there's noticeably more spice and slightly more oak. It has what I'd describe as a "dirtier" profile—not in a negative way, but in the sense that it feels more traditional, robust, and expressive.

For an everyday pour, that's exactly what I want.

---

# Finish

Medium to long.

Oak, pepper, and baking spice linger while the sweetness gradually fades.

The finish is slightly drier than Kirkland Bottled-in-Bond and leaves behind more barrel character.

---

# Overall Impression

Evan Williams Bottled-in-Bond continues to remind me that price doesn't determine quality.

It isn't flashy.

It isn't trendy.

It simply delivers honest Kentucky bourbon exactly as Bottled-in-Bond intended.

Every time I revisit it, I'm reminded why it remains one of the strongest values in bourbon.

# What I'd Change

Honestly...

Very little.

A few extra years in the barrel would certainly add complexity, but that's an unrealistic expectation at this price point.

---

# Comparison Notes

Compared with Kirkland Signature Bottled-in-Bond:

- More spice
- More oak
- More traditional Kentucky character
- Slightly rougher
- More expressive

Kirkland drinks smoother and leans slightly floral.

Evan Williams has more personality.

If I could only choose one budget Bottled-in-Bond bourbon, I'd still reach for Evan Williams.
