# Why I Bought It

After falling in love with Wild Turkey Rare Breed and Russell's Reserve Single Barrel, it only made sense to experience the bottle that sits at the heart of the Russell's lineup.

Russell's Reserve 10 Year isn't about barrel proof or flashy finishes.

It's about showcasing what ten years of patient aging can accomplish when paired with the classic Wild Turkey profile.

# About Russell's Reserve

Named after legendary Master Distiller **Jimmy Russell** and his son **Eddie Russell**, Russell's Reserve represents the premium side of the Wild Turkey family.

Unlike Rare Breed or Russell's Reserve Single Barrel, the 10 Year is bottled at an approachable 90 proof, allowing its mature oak and balanced bourbon character to take center stage.

---

# Appearance

Rich amber with golden copper highlights.

The extra years in the barrel show in both the color and the slow-moving legs coating the glass.

---

# Nose

Classic Wild Turkey, just more refined.

I found:

- Mature oak
- Caramel
- Vanilla
- Honey
- Orange peel
- Baking spices
- Toasted nuts
- Light leather

The aroma is elegant rather than aggressive.

---

# Palate

The first sip immediately highlights why age matters.

Notes include:

- Rich caramel
- Vanilla
- Toasted oak
- Honey
- Cinnamon
- Brown sugar
- Roasted peanuts
- Gentle black pepper

Everything feels integrated and mature.

The lower proof allows the flavors to unfold gradually without overwhelming the palate.

---

# Finish

Medium-long.

Oak, caramel, and baking spice linger before fading into soft pepper and dry barrel notes.

The finish is refined and balanced rather than bold.

---

# Overall Impression

Russell's Reserve 10 Year is bourbon for people who appreciate maturity over intensity.

It doesn't try to compete with barrel-proof bourbons.

Instead, it showcases just how enjoyable well-aged bourbon can be when bottled at an approachable proof.

---

# What I Liked

- Beautiful mature oak
- Excellent balance
- Very approachable
- Refined Wild Turkey profile
- Great everyday sipper
- Consistent quality

---

# What I'd Change

For my palate, I'd enjoy a little more proof.

Around 100 proof would likely elevate the richness and mouthfeel while preserving the excellent balance.

---

# Comparison Notes

### Compared with Wild Turkey 101

- Older oak profile
- More refined
- Less spice
- Smoother finish

### Compared with Wild Turkey Rare Breed

- Rare Breed has significantly more richness
- Rare Breed offers deeper caramel
- Rare Breed has greater viscosity
- Russell's 10 is easier to sip
- Russell's 10 emphasizes maturity over intensity

### Compared with Russell's Reserve Single Barrel

- Single Barrel has more complexity
- Single Barrel has richer oak
- Longer finish
- Higher proof
- Russell's 10 is softer and more approachable

# Buy Again?

## ✅ Yes

Russell's Reserve 10 Year is an easy recommendation.

Even with stronger bottles on my shelf, it earns its place through consistency and refinement.

---

# Final Thoughts

Russell's Reserve 10 Year reminded me that proof isn't everything.

While my palate naturally gravitates toward bottles like Rare Breed and Russell's Reserve Single Barrel, this bottle offers something different.

It demonstrates how patience and proper aging can produce a bourbon that's smooth, mature, and exceptionally well balanced.

It's an outstanding introduction to the premium Wild Turkey lineup and one I'd confidently recommend to almost anyone looking to move beyond entry-level bourbon.

---

# Rating

**8.9 / 10**

A refined, oak-forward bourbon that showcases ten years of maturation with excellent balance. It may not have the power of its higher-proof siblings, but it delivers classic Wild Turkey character in one of its most approachable forms.
