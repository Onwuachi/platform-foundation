# Derrick's Bourbon Journey

*From "more proof is more better" to everyday glory.*

A year of pours, palate shifts, and one $38 bottle that quietly became the standard everything else has to beat.

<!--more-->

> **"That oak is there and not bitter."**
> — the line that became the whole philosophy

---

## The Evolution

Five stages, roughly chronological, tracking how the chase changed shape — from chasing intensity to chasing balance.

### Stage 1 — Foundation
**Sweet, easy, and proof-curious**

The entry point. Vanilla and caramel-forward pours that taught the basics before any real opinions had formed.

- Evan Williams BiB
- Wild Turkey 101

### Stage 2 — Discovering Balance
**The first "this just works" bottle**

Elijah Craig Small Batch set an early bar — good, but eventually exposed as a touch thin and young once richer pours entered the picture.

- Elijah Craig Small Batch

### Stage 3 — Intensity & Experimentation
**Chasing the wild side**

Higher proof, bigger personalities, single barrels and double oaks. This is where "proof isn't everything" started to sink in — Rare Breed drank easier than its number suggested, and Sazerac Rye ("Baby Saz") delivered the year's clearest miss: grassy, herbal, ranked below Jack Daniel's banana-nail-polish note.

- Wild Turkey Rare Breed ⭐
- Russell's Reserve 10 Year
- Russell's Reserve Single Barrel
- Old Forester 1910
- Old Forester 1920
- Bardstown BiB 6 Year
- ~~Sazerac Rye~~ — pass

### Stage 4 — Mature Bourbon
**The standard quietly shifts**

A $38 7-year Bottled-in-Bond starts beating bottles two and three times its price. Not louder. Not rarer. Just correct, every single time.

- Heaven Hill 7 Year BiB ⭐ — *new standard pour*
- Knob Creek 9 Year — confirmed great
- Knob Creek 12 Year — confirmed great
- Knob Creek Blender's Edition 01 — confirmed great, 10yr-minimum blend
- Henry McKenna BiB
- Bondstone Double Oak

### Stage 5 — Dessert Bourbon
**Discovering the second-barrel obsession**

Woodford Double Oaked arrives as a genuine surprise — toffee, dark raisin, oak that's big but never bitter. The phrase that ends up defining the whole palate gets said here for the first time.

- Woodford Reserve Double Oaked ⭐

## The Bourbon Mount Rushmore

If the shelf burned down tomorrow, these are the four bottles getting replaced first — no debate.

| Rank | Bottle | Why |
|---|---|---|
| 1 — The Standard | **Heaven Hill 7yr Bottled-in-Bond** | Peanut brittle, cinnamon, caramel, oily mouthfeel, 100 proof, zero flaws. $38 and somehow still winning. |
| 2 — The Dessert King | **Woodford Reserve Double Oaked** | Toffee, vanilla cream, dark raisin, toasted oak — rich without ever tipping into bitter. |
| 3 — The Old-School King | **Russell's Reserve Single Barrel** | Dark cherry, orange peel, baking spice — tastes like someone built an Old Fashioned into the barrel. |
| 4 — The Wild Card | **Old Forester 1920 Prohibition Style** | Dark cherry, chocolate, brown sugar, viscous — proved 1910 wasn't a one-off. |

## The Bourbon DNA

A year of notes distilled into the rule set that actually predicts what gets a second pour.

**Always Wins**
- Mature oak that never turns bitter
- Caramel, toffee, peanut brittle
- Thick, oily mouthfeel
- Dark fruit — cherry, raisin
- Cinnamon and baking spice, in balance
- 95–110 proof sweet spot
- A long, clean finish

**Always Loses**
- Grassy, herbal rye spice
- Thin or watery texture
- Sharp young ethanol heat
- Artificial cherry sweetness
- Bitter, over-extracted oak
- Cold-filtered lightness
