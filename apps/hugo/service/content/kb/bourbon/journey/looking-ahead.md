# Looking Ahead

One of the biggest surprises of this journey is realizing that I no longer shop for bourbon the way I did when I started.

A year ago, my shopping list was driven by hype.

Today it's driven by curiosity.

Instead of asking,

"What's allocated?"

I ask,

"What can this bottle teach me?"

Sometimes the answer comes from a $120 bottle.

Sometimes it comes from a $25 Bottled in Bond bourbon sitting on the bottom shelf.

I've learned not to underestimate either.

---

# Bottles I'm Most Curious About

These aren't bottles I want because they're difficult to find.

They're bottles that I believe have something new to teach me.

## Old Forester 1924

Probably the bottle I'm most curious about today.

Ten-year age statement.

Dark graham cracker.

Fudge.

Cocoa.

Cured tobacco.

Everything about the profile suggests it could fit my palate perfectly.

The only obstacle is the price.

---

## Russell's Reserve 13

If Russell's Reserve Single Barrel represents elegance...

What happens when that profile gets several more years in the barrel?

That's the question I want answered.

---

## Wild Turkey Jimmy Russell 70th Anniversary

Wild Turkey has become one of my favorite distillery styles.

This bottle represents decades of one of bourbon's greatest master distillers.

---

## Elijah Craig 18

Can older Heaven Hill oak remain balanced?

Or does it become too much?

Only one way to find out.

---

## Weller Full Proof

Not because it's allocated.

Because I genuinely want to understand how a wheated full-proof bourbon compares to the flavor profile I've already developed.

---

## Peerless Double Oak

Everything I've read suggests this could be an incredible match for my palate.

Unfortunately, so does the price tag.

---

# Lessons Learned

If I could go back and give myself advice on day one, it would be this.

Don't chase hype.

Don't chase proof.

Don't chase age.

Don't chase allocated bottles.

Learn distilleries.

Learn mash bills.

Learn Bottled in Bond.

Learn DSP numbers.

Take notes.

Taste side by side.

Trust your palate.

And remember that price doesn't guarantee enjoyment.

Some of my favorite bottles cost less than forty dollars.

That's one of the best discoveries I've made.

---

# Final Thoughts

Bourbon has become much more than another hobby.

It's become another way to learn.

Every bottle tells a story.

Every comparison teaches something new.

Every tasting refines my palate just a little more.

This journal isn't really about ranking whiskey.

It's about documenting the process of learning to appreciate it.

That's why this page will never truly be finished.

As new bottles are opened...

As old favorites are revisited...

As my palate continues to evolve...

This knowledge base will evolve with it.

And years from now, I hope I can look back at these notes and see not just which bourbons I enjoyed...

...but how I learned to enjoy them.
