# Derrick's Top 5 Bourbon Shelf

As my bourbon journey has evolved, I've stopped chasing allocated bottles and internet hype.

Instead, I've focused on finding bourbons that consistently deliver the flavors I enjoy most: rich oak, caramel, balanced spice, good mouthfeel, and excellent value.

This list represents the bottles that have earned a permanent place on my shelf.

---

# 🥇 1. Wild Turkey Rare Breed

**Role:** Daily Premium Pour

Why it's here:

- Exceptional value
- Barrel Proof without excessive heat
- Rich caramel and dark fruit
- Classic Wild Turkey spice
- Incredible balance

If I could only keep one bourbon on my shelf, this would probably be it.

---

# 🥈 2. Russell's Reserve Single Barrel

**Role:** Elevated Single Barrel Experience

Why it's here:

- More refined than Rare Breed
- Excellent oak integration
- Cherry, caramel, vanilla
- Long finish
- Every pour feels special

This is Wild Turkey with another layer of complexity.

---

# 🥉 3. Old Forester 1920

**Role:** Brown-Forman Benchmark

Why it's here:

- Dark chocolate
- Cherry
- Rich oak
- Brown sugar
- Excellent viscosity

1920 showed me just how good the Brown-Forman profile can be.

---

# 4. Heaven Hill Bottled in Bond

**Role:** Benchmark Bottled-in-Bond Bourbon

Why it's here:

- Incredible value
- Balanced oak and spice
- Traditional bourbon profile
- Consistently enjoyable
- My reference point for comparing other Bottled-in-Bond bourbons

This is the bottle I mentally compare almost everything else against.

---

# 5. Woodford Reserve Double Oaked

**Role:** Dessert / Evening Pour

Why it's here:

- Rich toasted oak
- Chocolate
- Caramel
- Marshmallow
- Silky mouthfeel

Few bourbons deliver this much dessert character while remaining balanced.

---

# What I've Learned About My Palate

After reviewing my favorite bottles, some clear patterns have emerged.

I consistently enjoy:

- Higher proof (100+)
- Rich oak
- Caramel
- Brown sugar
- Dark fruit
- Good mouthfeel
- Long finishes
- Well-integrated spice

I generally enjoy less:

- Young grain-forward bourbon
- Thin mouthfeel
- Artificial sweetness
- Overly floral profiles
- Excessive ethanol

# Final Thoughts

One of the biggest lessons I've learned is that great bourbon isn't always the most expensive bourbon.

Some of my favorite pours are readily available bottles that consistently deliver exceptional quality and value.

My goal isn't to own the rarest collection.

It's to build a shelf of bottles I'll genuinely look forward to pouring.
