# Derrick's Top 5 Bourbons (2026)

Over the past year my bourbon journey has changed dramatically.

When I first started exploring bourbon, I was focused on finding highly allocated bottles and chasing recommendations from YouTube, Reddit, and whiskey forums.

Today my philosophy is much simpler.

I'm no longer searching for the rarest bottle.

I'm searching for the bottles I genuinely enjoy drinking.

These five bourbons have consistently earned their place on my shelf because they deliver the flavors I value most:

- Rich oak
- Caramel
- Brown sugar
- Balanced spice
- Excellent mouthfeel
- Long finishes
- Outstanding value

This list isn't intended to tell anyone what the "best" bourbon is.

It's a snapshot of where my palate stands today.

# Understanding the Ratings

These aren't review scores.

They're characteristics that matter most to my palate.

- **Oak** — Toasted oak, vanilla, barrel influence, char.
- **Sweetness** — Caramel, brown sugar, honey, dark fruit.
- **Spice** — Rye spice, cinnamon, pepper.
- **Proof** — How well the alcohol complements the whiskey.
- **Value** — Overall quality relative to price.
- **Shelf Role** — Why this bottle exists in my collection.

---

# 🥇 Wild Turkey Rare Breed

**Role:** My Benchmark Bourbon

If someone asked me to recommend one bourbon that represents everything I enjoy, Rare Breed would probably be my answer.

It combines bold oak, dark caramel, baking spice, dark fruit, and barrel-proof intensity while remaining remarkably balanced.

No bottle in my collection offers a better combination of flavor, proof, and value.

**Why it's here**

- Outstanding value
- Rich oak
- Excellent balance
- Powerful without drinking hot
- Consistently available

---

# 🥈 Russell's Reserve Single Barrel

**Role:** My Favorite Single Barrel

Russell's Single Barrel takes everything I love about Rare Breed and adds another level of refinement.

Every barrel has its own personality, but the core Wild Turkey profile always shines through.

Cherry, vanilla, caramel, and mature oak create one of the most elegant pours on my shelf.

---

# 🥉 Old Forester 1920

**Role:** Brown-Forman Benchmark

Old Forester 1920 completely changed my opinion of Brown-Forman bourbon.

Dark chocolate.

Brown sugar.

Cherry.

Rich toasted oak.

Dense mouthfeel.

This bottle showed me how rich and layered the Brown-Forman profile can become.

---

# 4. Heaven Hill Bottled in Bond

**Role:** Everyday Benchmark

If Rare Breed is my premium benchmark...

Heaven Hill Bottled in Bond is my everyday benchmark.

It's affordable.

Consistent.

Traditional.

Balanced.

Whenever I review another Bottled-in-Bond bourbon, this is usually the bottle I'm mentally comparing it against.

---

# 5. Woodford Reserve Double Oaked

**Role:** Dessert Pour

No other bottle on my shelf satisfies a dessert craving quite like Woodford Double Oaked.

Heavy toasted oak.

Chocolate.

Marshmallow.

Vanilla.

Caramel.

It's rich without becoming cloying and remains one of the smoothest pours I own.

---

# What My Top Five Says About My Palate

Looking across these bottles, several patterns emerge.

I consistently gravitate toward bourbons that emphasize:

- Mature oak
- Caramel
- Brown sugar
- Dark fruit
- Rich viscosity
- Long finishes
- Proof around 100–116

I generally enjoy less:

- Thin mouthfeel
- Young grain-forward whiskey
- Artificial sweetness
- Excessive floral notes
- Overly hot ethanol

Understanding these preferences has made buying bourbon much more intentional.

Instead of purchasing bottles because they're popular, I now buy bottles that answer questions about my palate.

# Final Thoughts

One lesson has become increasingly clear throughout this journey.

Great bourbon isn't defined by rarity.

It isn't defined by hype.

And it certainly isn't defined by price alone.

The best bourbon is the one that makes you want another pour.

For me, these five bottles have earned that privilege.

I'm sure this list will evolve over time, but that's part of the journey.

Every new bottle teaches me something.

Every review refines my palate.

And every ranking is simply a snapshot of where that journey stands today.
