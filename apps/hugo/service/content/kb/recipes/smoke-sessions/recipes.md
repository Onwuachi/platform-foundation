# Recipes

The Recipes section is a collection of operational runbooks for barbecue and smoked foods.

These are not traditional recipes.

They are **repeatable cooking procedures** designed to produce consistent results using objective decision points rather than fixed cook times.

---

## Structure

Recipes are organized into four categories:

### 🔥 Smoke Sessions
Complete end-to-end cooks that combine multiple proteins and timing coordination.

Examples:
- Overnight Brisket
- Brisket + St. Louis Ribs
- Holiday Turkey Smoke

---

### 🥩 Proteins
Canonical procedures for individual meats.

Examples:
- Brisket
- St. Louis Style Ribs
- Pork Butt
- Salmon

---

### 🧰 Techniques
Reusable cooking methods used across multiple recipes.

Examples:
- Dry Brining
- Beef Tallow Rendering
- Butcher Paper Wrap
- Resting Meat

---

### 📚 Reference
Static knowledge and charts used across all cooking.

Examples:
- Pellet Guide
- Wood Selection
- Internal Temperature Guide
- Meat Doneness Criteria

---

## Philosophy

- Cook to **tenderness**, not time
- Bark is validated, not assumed
- Resting is part of the cook
- Each session is an **operation**, not a recipe
- Every cook should improve the system
