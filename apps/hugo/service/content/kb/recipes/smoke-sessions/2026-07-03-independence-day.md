# US 2026 Independence Day Smoke Session

> Production execution log for the July 3, 2026 holiday smoke.

---

# Objectives

Primary goals:

- Produce a Texas-style brisket.
- Smoke two racks of St. Louis style ribs.
- Render beef tallow from brisket trim.
- Validate several improvements to the Recipes knowledge base.

---

# Equipment

- Traeger Woodridge Pro
- Wireless meat probes
- Butcher paper
- Cooling cooler for hot hold
- Mason jars for rendered tallow

---

# Fuel Strategy

## Primary Smoke Phase

Used premium smoke pellets:

- Smokin' Pecan Pellets
- B&B Championship Blend

Purpose:

Maximize smoke flavor during the period when the meat accepts smoke most efficiently.

---

## Finish Phase

After wrapping:

Switched to Kirkland Hardwood Pellets.

Reason:

Once wrapped, additional smoke contributes very little to flavor.

Premium pellets are reserved for the bark formation phase while inexpensive pellets provide efficient heat during the finish.

**Decision:** Adopt this as the standard pellet strategy.

---

# Meat Preparation

## Brisket

- Dry brined overnight
- Final seasoning applied prior to smoking
- Wrapped in butcher paper with rendered beef tallow

---

## St. Louis Ribs

- Dry brined overnight
- Seasoned prior to smoking

Two racks were intentionally cooked using different finishing methods.

---

# Timeline

## Early Morning

Smoker operating at 225°F.

Brisket entered the stall normally.

Ribs were added later in the morning after approximately one hour at room temperature.

Super Smoke enabled during the early smoke phase.

---

## Brisket Wrap

Brisket wrapped at approximately **165°F**.

Added rendered beef tallow inside the butcher paper.

Temperature increased to **250°F** after wrapping.

---

## Rib Experiment

### Rack A

- Smoked unwrapped
- Glazed late
- Returned to smoker unwrapped to set glaze
- Wrapped only after glaze bonded

---

### Rack B

- Wrapped earlier
- Finished in butcher paper
- Unwrapped near completion
- Final glaze applied near the end

This became the primary experiment of the cook.

---

## Final Cooking Phase

Temperature increased to approximately **255°F** for the final portion of the cook.

---

# Operational Incident

## Unexpected Traeger Shutdown

The smoker unexpectedly entered its shutdown cycle during the cook.

The shutdown was noticed immediately because smoke production stopped and chamber temperature began falling.

Recovery actions:

- Did **not** open the lid.
- Allowed chamber heat to stabilize.
- Restarted the smoker.
- Returned to cooking temperature.
- Continued normal operation.

Maximum observed temperature loss was minimal and no noticeable impact occurred to the final product.

**Lesson Learned**

Do not panic.

Opening the lid would have caused significantly greater heat loss than allowing the smoker to recover naturally.

---

# Rib Experiment Results

## Rack A

Late glaze followed by glaze set on the grate.

Results:

- Excellent bark retention
- Beautiful glaze adhesion
- Firm exterior
- Traditional bite

---

## Rack B

Wrapped earlier with glaze finishing near completion.

Results:

- Slightly softer exterior
- Excellent moisture
- Outstanding tenderness

Initial tasting was extremely positive.

Additional side-by-side comparisons will determine whether this becomes the preferred finishing method.

---

# Brisket Results

Final pull decision was based on **probe tenderness**, not temperature alone.

Observations:

- Point section became probe tender first.
- Flat remained slightly firmer.
- Internal temperature remained only a guide.
- Probe feel ultimately determined removal.

Brisket transferred to insulated cooler for hot hold.

Planned slicing temperature:

Approximately **140°F** internal.

---

# Beef Tallow Production

Rendered brisket fat was strained after the cook.

Final yield:

- **Two 3/4-cup mason jars**

Total rendered tallow:

**1½ cups**

This tallow will be reserved for future brisket wraps and other beef cooks.

---

# Major Lessons Learned

## 1. Smoke Early

Premium pellets provide the greatest benefit before wrapping.

After wrapping, economical hardwood pellets are sufficient.

---

## 2. Probe Tenderness Wins

Internal temperature remains a guide.

Probe feel determines completion.

---

## 3. Toothpick Testing

The toothpick test proved extremely useful for judging rib tenderness.

This will become a standard validation step.

---

## 4. Bark Before Wrap

Wrapping decisions should be based on bark development and tenderness—not a specific temperature.

---

## 5. Two Different Rib Finishes Both Work

Both finishing methods produced excellent ribs.

Future cooks should continue refining the preferred approach.

---

## 6. Stay Calm During Equipment Issues

The unexpected Traeger shutdown reinforced an important lesson:

Do not chase temperatures.

Avoid opening the lid.

Restore normal operation and continue cooking.
