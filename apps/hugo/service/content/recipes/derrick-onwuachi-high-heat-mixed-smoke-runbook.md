# Derrick Onwuachi's High Heat Mixed Smoke Runbook

### Beef Plate Ribs • Pork Spare Ribs • Chuck Roast • Salmon

**Date Validated:** June 20, 2026
**Location:** Apple Valley, Minnesota
**Status:** ✅ Production Approved — First Fully Validated Onwuachi High Heat Mixed Smoke Cook

---

## 🎛️ Core Philosophy

The old rules are optional.

The June 20 cook proved:

* Smoke adhesion first, heat acceleration early.
* Butcher paper over heavy foil.
* Smoked tallow as a wrap enhancer, not a crutch.
* Resting is an active cooking phase.
* Finish in hours, not all day.

### Primary Operating Temperature

## **275°F**

This is now the official **Onwuachi High Heat Standard**.

Not:

* 225°F all day
* 12+ hour cooks
* Chasing perfect ambient temperatures

The goal:

* Faster rendering
* Excellent bark
* Reduced stall time
* Predictable finish windows

### Emergency Range

```text
285°F - 300°F
```

Reserved only for:

* Severe stalls
* Behind-schedule cooks
* Emergency finish acceleration

**275°F remains the production default.**

---

## 🧈 Phase 0 — Smoked Beef Tallow

Before loading meat:

Trim excess fat from:

* Beef plate ribs
* Chuck roast trimmings

Render in:

```text
Covered cast iron pan
```

### Target

* Golden liquid
* Light bubbling
* Rich smoke aroma
* Lightly browned cracklings

### Used For

* Beef wrap
* Pork wrap
* Chuck wrap

### Not Used For

* Salmon

> "If the smoker is running, the tallow pan is too."

---

# 🥩 Beef Plate Ribs — King of the Pit

### Phase 1

```text
225°F
Super Smoke
30-45 Minutes
```

Purpose:

* Smoke adhesion
* Bark foundation
* Color development

### Heat Push

Raise pit to:

```text
275°F
```

Continue until:

* Internal 170–175°F

OR

* Bark dark mahogany
* Dry surface
* Rub does not wipe off

### Wrap

```text
Pink Butcher Paper

+

1-2 tbsp Smoked Tallow
```

### Finish

Continue at:

```text
275°F
```

Pull:

```text
200-205°F
```

Probe:

> Like warm butter.

### Actual June 20 Results

```text
8:30 AM    Beef Plate On

11:00 AM   172°F

11:00 AM   Wrapped

11:28 AM   190°F
```

Observation:

The combination of:

* Early smoke adhesion
* 275°F pit
* Butcher paper
* Smoked tallow

dramatically reduced stall severity while preserving bark.

**This cook officially retired the old 225°F-all-morning approach.**

# 🐖 Pork Spare Ribs

### Cook

```text
275°F
```

No foil.

No spritz rituals.

No sugar baths.

### Glaze Phase

At:

```text
145-155°F
```

Apply:

* Diana Sauce
* Vinegar
* Seasoning blend

Very thin coat.

Tack only.

### Wrap

```text
Pink Butcher Paper

+

1 tbsp Smoked Tallow

+

Small Amount Glaze
```

### Finish

```text
195-200°F
```

Primary indicators:

* Bend test
* Probe tenderness

Temperature confirms.

Texture decides.

# 🥩 Chuck Roast — Mini Brisket

Cook:

```text
275°F
```

* Bark

Wrap:

```text
165-175°F

OR

When Bark Is Visually Set
```

```text
Pink Butcher Paper

+

2 tbsp Smoked Tallow
```

Finish:

```text
198-205°F
```

Probe tender.

```text
30-45 Minutes
```

# 🐟 Salmon Boat Method v3.0

## Final Lessons Learned

Do NOT automatically reduce pit temperature.

If the smoker is already stable at:

```text
275°F
```

Leave it there.

The butcher paper boat protects the fish perfectly.

---

### Boat Construction

* Lemon slices underneath
* Salmon skin-side down
* Lemon slices on top
* Salt
* Pepper
* Garlic powder
* Light olive oil

Fold sides upward.

Leave:

```text
Top Open
```

---

### Fat Choice

Preferred:

```text
Light Olive Oil
```

Avoid:

```text
Smoked Beef Tallow
```

Reason:

Salmon already contains abundant natural oils.

Olive oil preserves:

* Bright lemon flavor
* Cleaner smoke profile
* Better contrast alongside beef and pork

---

### Cook

```text
275°F
```

Check:

```text
30 Minutes
```

Pull:

```text
135-140°F
```

Rest:

```text
10 Minutes
```

## ✅ Final Verdict

The June 20 smoke officially validated:

* **275°F** as the primary pit temperature.
* Smoked tallow as a wrap enhancer for beef, pork, and chuck.
* Beef plate ribs finish dramatically faster at higher heat.
* Chuck roast behaves reliably as a mini brisket.
* Pork spare ribs retain bark beautifully in butcher paper.
* Salmon performs best at ambient pit temperature with olive oil and lemon.
* 285–300°F remains the emergency recovery range, not the standard.
